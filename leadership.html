Leadership

Meet the traditional rulers, chiefs, and queenmothers who continue to uphold the values, customs, and development of Bowiri Traditional Area.

Paramount Chief

Nana Osiadieyor Kwabena Salo V

Born on 5th October, 1999 at Bowiri Anyinase in the Bayida Clan, Biakoye District, Oti Region. He began his primary education at Worawora Anglican Primary School and completed JHS in 2017. He attended Bishop Herman College (2017–2020), taught for four years at Stars On Earth Academy, Accra Madina, and is now a student of Peki College of Education pursuing a Bachelor’s Degree in Education. He was enstooled on 13th October, 2023 as the Paramount Chief of Bowiri Traditional Area (Bowirimanhene). Nana is an organist and enjoys soft music.

Sub-Divisional Chief

Nana Kwasi Asiedu II

Born on 2nd December 1973 to Inspector John Kofi Anso Bandua and Madam Gertrude Theodora Abena Asiedu-Bandua, both of Bowiri Anyinase. Educated in Kumasi and later worked as a driver for KBL (1989–1991) and with the Ministry of Defence (1997–present). Enstooled on 19th November, 2021 as Sub-Divisional Chief, with private name Jacob Kwasi Anso Bandua. He serves as Ankobehene of Bowiri Traditional Area, from the Bawunya Clan.

Tufuhene of Bowiri Traditional Area

Nana Kwasi Okyerefor III

Born on 11th March, 1969 at Bowiri Aboabo. Attended Bowiri Takrabe L/A Middle School, later worked with Abibigroma Theatre Company and National Theatre, Accra. He travelled to the USA in 1997, where he gained vast experience in transport logistics and later founded Efo Ike Logistics Inc in 2016. Enstooled on 13th August, 2021 as the Tufuhene of Bowiri Traditional Area.

Kyidomhene of Bowiri Traditional Area

Nana Kwasi Sike II

Born on 25th December 1984 at Bowiri Kwamekrom. Studied Building Technology at Ho Technical University and Quantity Surveying at KNUST. Worked with various Municipal Assemblies as Technical Engineer and Quantity Surveyor. Enstooled on 5th August, 2022 as the Chief of Bowiri Kwamekrom with the title Kyidomhene. Married with three children, he enjoys singing and fostering positive relationships.

Gyasehene of Bowiri Traditional Area

Nana Kwasi Asantey II

Born in 1976 to Mr. Odeifor Kwasi John and Madam Afua Victoria Oheneasah of Bowiri Amanfrom. Educated at Bowiri Amanfrom E.P. JHS and trained as a carpenter. Married with seven children, he was enstooled on 18th November, 2022 as the Gyasehene of Bowiri Kwamekrom. He is both a craftsman and a businessman, serving his community with dedication.

Queenmothers

Nana Yaa Sikebea II — Kyidomhemaa

Born on 9th April, 1964 at Bowiri Kwamekrom to Mr. Kofi Kumah Bediako and Madam Afua Atukpawu. Began primary education at Bowiri Kwamekrom E.P. School in 1970, later moved to Bowiri Amanfrom. Enstooled on 5th August, 2022 as Kyidomhemaa at Bowiri Kwamekrom. A strong and respected leader among the women of the area.

Nana Yaa Asiedua I

Born on 3rd September, 1981 at Bowiri Anyinase to Mr. Solomon G.Y. Asiedu and Mrs. Mary Abena Asiedu. Educated at Sogakofe and Ho Mawuli Senior High School; obtained “O” Level in 1999. She is a former athlete and self-employed businesswoman, married with four children. Enstooled as Queenmother of Bowiri Anyinase; she loves soft music and film.
